# LinkedIn Positioning AI — Conversion-Driven MVP User Journey

## 1) High-level journey map

## 1. Acquisition
**Intention:** attract qualified intent traffic (people actively wanting better LinkedIn outcomes).  
**Goal:** make promise instantly clear and relevant.

## 2. Landing
**Intention:** reduce skepticism in first 10 seconds.  
**Goal:** answer: “Is this for me?”, “What do I get?”, “How fast?”, “Can I trust this?”

## 3. Decision
**Intention:** remove uncertainty and decision friction.  
**Goal:** one clear offer, one CTA, concrete outcome.

## 4. Purchase
**Intention:** complete payment with minimal fields and no distraction.  
**Goal:** fast checkout confidence (PIX/card/boleto clarity).

## 5. Onboarding
**Intention:** orient user immediately after purchase.  
**Goal:** tell user exactly what will happen and how long it takes.

## 6. Input Collection
**Intention:** collect enough context for high-quality output without fatigue.  
**Goal:** short guided multi-step flow.

## 7. Processing
**Intention:** transform waiting into perceived value.  
**Goal:** show progress and strategic intelligence, avoid anxiety.

## 8. Results Delivery
**Intention:** create immediate “this is valuable” reaction.  
**Goal:** clear structure + quick wins + copy-ready output.

## 9. Upsell
**Intention:** present deeper support only after value moment.  
**Goal:** natural continuation, not pressure.

## 10. Retention
**Intention:** bring users back to apply, update, and generate again.  
**Goal:** habit hooks and practical reminders.

---

## 2) Golden path (primary user flow)

Design rule: one clear action per step.

| Step | Screen | Purpose | User Action | System Response | Emotional State |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Landing | Establish trust + value | Click primary CTA | Opens checkout | Curious + hopeful |
| 2 | Checkout | Convert interest into purchase | Complete payment | Confirms payment instantly | Committed but cautious |
| 3 | Onboarding Intro | Set expectations | Click “Start diagnosis” | Opens intake step 1 | Oriented + calmer |
| 4 | Intake Steps | Collect strategic inputs | Answer step questions | Saves progress + advances | Guided, low effort |
| 5 | Intake Confirmation | Final check before generation | Click “Generate now” | Queues AI generation | Anticipation |
| 6 | Generation Loading | Maintain trust while waiting | Stay / enable notification | Shows staged progress | Curious, not anxious |
| 7 | Results Dashboard | Deliver core value | Review sections + copy content | Highlights quick wins | Validated + impressed |
| 8 | Report View/PDF | Increase perceived professionalism | Download/share PDF | Generates/downloads report | Proud + practical |
| 9 | Upsell Offer | Extend value | Buy deeper review (optional) | Creates upsell order | Open to next step |
|10 | Return Loop | Encourage re-engagement | Revisit via reminder | Shows actions/history | Supported |

Always-visible “what happens next” pattern:
- Top status stepper on key screens.
- “Next step” card at bottom of each major page.

## 3) Screen-by-screen breakdown

## 3.1 Landing Page
- **Goal:** turn attention into trust and click.
- **Main message:** fast, strategic LinkedIn transformation.
- **Primary CTA:** `Quero melhorar meu LinkedIn agora`
- **Secondary actions:** `Ver exemplo de resultado` (only one secondary).
- **Content structure:**
  1. Hero promise
  2. Social proof
  3. Before/after snippet
  4. How it works (3 steps)
  5. Offer + CTA
- **Microcopy (PT-BR):**
  - “Seu LinkedIn pode abrir portas em poucos minutos.”
  - “Receba um diagnóstico estratégico + textos prontos para aplicar.”
- **Avoid:** feature list overload, multiple competing CTAs.

## 3.2 Checkout
- **Goal:** finalize purchase with confidence.
- **Main message:** secure payment, immediate start.
- **Primary CTA:** `Finalizar compra`
- **Secondary actions:** `Voltar`
- **Content structure:** offer summary, payment methods, guarantee, CTA.
- **Microcopy (PT-BR):**
  - “Pagamento seguro. Acesso imediato após confirmação.”
  - “Você começa seu diagnóstico em menos de 2 minutos.”
- **Avoid:** distractions, long legal text before CTA.

## 3.3 Onboarding Intro
- **Goal:** orient and reduce cognitive friction.
- **Main message:** this is quick and guided.
- **Primary CTA:** `Começar diagnóstico`
- **Secondary actions:** none.
- **Content structure:** welcome + 3 bullets (time, steps, outcome).
- **Microcopy (PT-BR):**
  - “Vamos fazer isso juntos, passo a passo.”
  - “Tempo médio: 4 a 6 minutos.”
- **Avoid:** asking profile data before context-setting.

## 3.4 Intake Form (multi-step)
- **Goal:** collect only high-impact context.
- **Main message:** each answer improves quality of output.
- **Primary CTA:** `Continuar`
- **Secondary actions:** `Voltar`.
- **Content structure:** progress bar, 1 topic per screen, examples, helper text.
- **Microcopy (PT-BR):**
  - “Sem resposta perfeita. Responda como você falaria em uma entrevista.”
  - “Isso ajuda a IA a escrever no seu contexto real.”
- **Avoid:** long page with many fields, jargon-heavy language.

## 3.5 Loading / Generation
- **Goal:** convert waiting into trust and anticipation.
- **Main message:** strategic analysis is in progress.
- **Primary CTA:** `Avise-me por e-mail` (if user leaves)
- **Secondary actions:** `Continuar aqui`
- **Content structure:** staged status + value hints + expected time.
- **Microcopy (PT-BR):**
  - “Estamos analisando seu posicionamento profissional.”
  - “Próximo passo: otimizar seu título e resumo.”
- **Avoid:** static spinner without context.

## 3.6 Results Dashboard
- **Goal:** deliver immediate value and actionability.
- **Main message:** your new positioning is ready.
- **Primary CTA:** `Aplicar no LinkedIn agora`
- **Secondary actions:** `Copiar seção`, `Baixar PDF`
- **Content structure:**
  1. Diagnosis summary
  2. Headline
  3. About
  4. Experience rewrites
  5. 5 first post ideas
  6. Next step card
- **Microcopy (PT-BR):**
  - “Seu diagnóstico está pronto. Comece pelo título para ganho rápido.”
  - “Copie e aplique em poucos minutos.”
- **Avoid:** dump all text in one giant block.

## 3.7 Report View (PDF)
- **Goal:** increase authority and perceived quality.
- **Main message:** structured professional report.
- **Primary CTA:** `Baixar relatório em PDF`
- **Secondary actions:** `Enviar para meu e-mail`
- **Microcopy (PT-BR):**
  - “Seu plano de posicionamento em formato profissional.”
- **Avoid:** visual inconsistency with dashboard.

## 3.8 Upsell Screen
- **Goal:** offer next level only after value proof.
- **Main message:** expert review amplifies results.
- **Primary CTA:** `Quero revisão premium`
- **Secondary actions:** `Agora não`
- **Microcopy (PT-BR):**
  - “Quer ir além? Um especialista revisa seu posicionamento final.”
- **Avoid:** fear-based pressure messaging.

## 4) Intake flow design (critical)

## Flow sequence (6 steps)

1. **Objective & target**
   - Questions:
     - “Qual é seu principal objetivo no LinkedIn hoje?” (required)
     - “Qual cargo/posição você quer atrair?” (required)

2. **Current profile context**
   - Questions:
     - “Qual é seu título atual no LinkedIn?” (required)
     - “Cole a URL do seu LinkedIn” (optional)

3. **Experience highlights**
   - Questions:
     - “Quais 2–3 resultados você mais se orgulha?” (required)
     - “Quais competências quer reforçar?” (optional)

4. **Audience and niche**
   - Questions:
     - “Quem você quer atrair?” (recrutadores, clientes, parceiros) (required)
     - “Em qual setor/nicho você atua?” (required)

5. **Tone and positioning preference**
   - Questions:
     - “Qual tom combina com você?” (Estratégico, Inspirador, Técnico, Executivo) (required)
     - “Palavras que quer evitar” (optional)

6. **Final review**
   - Show concise summary of answers.
   - Primary action: `Gerar meu diagnóstico`

## Progression logic
- 1 conceptual theme per screen.
- Max 2 required fields per step.
- Auto-advance only for objective radio/select questions.

## Validation
- Inline validation with plain language.
- Required fields clearly marked.
- Minimum text length for “results/proud moments” step.

## Required vs optional strategy
- Required only when directly impacting output quality.
- Optional fields always labeled as optional with benefit context.

## Tone of guidance
- Encouraging, non-judgmental, practical.
- Example pattern:
  - “Exemplo: ‘Liderei projeto que reduziu custos em 18%.’”

## 5) Loading & waiting experience

## What user sees
- 4-stage progress model:
  1. “Entendendo seu objetivo”
  2. “Analisando seu posicionamento atual”
  3. “Escrevendo sua versão otimizada”
  4. “Finalizando recomendações e posts”

## Perceived time design
- Show realistic estimate: “Tempo médio: 60–120 segundos”.
- Each stage gets a short explanation to signal active work.

## Feedback and anticipation
- Live stage transitions.
- “What you will receive” checklist appears beside loader.

## Anxiety reduction
- Never show blank screens.
- Offer email notification fallback:
  - `Receber aviso por e-mail quando estiver pronto`

## 6) Results experience (core value moment)

## Results sections (in order)
1. **Strategic diagnosis summary** (top)
2. **Optimized headline**
3. **Optimized About**
4. **Experience rewrites**
5. **Suggested positioning angle**
6. **5 first post ideas**
7. **Next action panel**

## Visual hierarchy
- Start with a concise “big insight” block.
- Use collapsible sections for detail depth.
- Keep copy buttons near each actionable block.

## Reading guidance
- Add labels like “Apply first”, “Quick win”, “Advanced improvement”.
- End each section with one clear user action.

## Overwhelm prevention
- Default to summary + one expanded section.
- Progressive disclosure for longer outputs.

## Next-action design
- Primary CTA on page: `Aplicar no LinkedIn agora`
- Bottom card suggests: PDF + upsell + reminder setup.

---

## 7) Upsell strategy inside the flow

## When to show
- Show **after** user sees full value moment (results loaded + at least one interaction).

## How to position
- Position as acceleration and confidence boost.
- “Você já tem uma ótima base. Quer elevar para nível especialista?”

## Trust-safe framing
- Optional by default.
- Clear scope, delivery time, and expected outcome.
- No artificial countdown if not real.

## Value connection
- Link upsell to specific observed gaps from diagnosis.

---

## 8) Retention & return behavior

## What makes users come back
- Action checklist (“Applied headline?”, “Updated About?”).
- Post-implementation follow-up prompts.

## Follow-up actions
- Day 1: apply profile rewrite reminder.
- Day 3: publish first post reminder.
- Day 7: “Want a second optimization pass?” prompt.

## Notifications
- Email first (MVP).
- WhatsApp optional later.

## Long-term hooks
- Saved result history.
- Re-run with new objective.
- Future content calendar upsell path.

---

## 9) UX principles (non-negotiable)

1. **One decision per screen.**
2. **One primary CTA per screen.**
3. **Always explicit next step.**
4. **No dead ends (every state has a recovery action).**
5. **Progressive disclosure over information dump.**
6. **Use plain language, never internal jargon.**
7. **Mobile-first interaction priority.**
8. **System should guide, not ask users to plan.**

---

## 10) Anti-patterns (what to avoid)

1. Long forms in one screen.
2. Multiple competing CTAs in hero and checkout.
3. Weak loading feedback (“just spinner”).
4. Dumping all AI output without prioritization.
5. Showing upsell before core value is felt.
6. Complex navigation during first-run flow.
7. Vague copy (“continue”, “submit”) without outcome framing.
8. Forcing user decisions when optional path is enough.

## B) 1-minute version (for founders)

User sees a clear promise, buys quickly, and is guided through a short step-by-step intake.  
While AI processes, the user sees meaningful staged progress (not a blank spinner).  
Results open in a structured dashboard where the first improvement is obvious and easy to apply.  
After value is proven, an optional premium review upsell appears as a natural next step.  
Follow-up reminders bring users back to implement and re-engage.

## D) 3 most critical moments for conversion success

1. **Landing clarity (first 10 seconds):** if value and trust are not immediate, user drops.
2. **Checkout confidence:** payment friction or uncertainty kills purchase completion.
3. **Results first impression:** if “wow + actionable” is weak, upsell and retention collapse.
